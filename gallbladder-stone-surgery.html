Gall Bladder Stone Surgery in Patna | Trusted Laparoscopic Surgeon

Gall Bladder Surgery in Patna involves removing a diseased gall bladder to treat problems caused by gallstones and inflammation. The procedure helps relieve pain, improve digestion, and restore overall comfort in daily life.

Although gallstones are the most common reason for Gall Bladder Surgery, it also treats infections, blockages, and other gall bladder disorders. Surgeons recommend this surgery when medications and lifestyle changes fail to provide lasting relief from symptoms. A Gall Bladder Surgeon in Patna uses two surgical approaches to perform gall bladder removal that many patients are not aware of.

Important Things Patients Should Know About Gall Bladder Surgery

1. Ways to Perform Gall Bladder Surgery

There are two main surgical approaches used by surgeons:

1. Laparoscopic Gall Bladder Surgery (Keyhole Surgery)
  • ✔ Most common and preferred method
  • ✔ Performed using small cuts and a camera
  • ✔ Less pain, minimal scars
  • ✔ Faster recovery (usually 2–5 days)
2. Open Gall Bladder Surgery
  • ✔ Used in complicated cases (severe infection, scarring, obesity, or previous surgeries)
  • ✔ Requires a larger incision
  • ✔ Longer hospital stay and recovery time
Note: Your surgeon decides the method based on your condition, scans, and medical history.

2. Who Needs Gall Bladder Surgery?

Gall bladder surgery is usually recommended if you have:

  • Recurrent gallstone pain
  • Gall bladder infection (cholecystitis)
  • Gall bladder inflammation
  • Blocked bile ducts
  • Complications like jaundice or pancreatitis

3. Is Gall Bladder Surgery Safe?

Yes, it is a very common and safe procedure when done by an experienced surgeon.

Complications are rare and mostly manageable.
  • Infection
  • Bleeding
  • Injury to bile duct
  • Temporary digestive discomfort

4. Recovery After Gall Bladder Surgery

  • Hospital stay: Same day or 1–2 days
  • Return to normal activities: 7–10 days
  • Full recovery: 2–3 weeks
  • Light diet advised initially
Most patients live a completely normal life without a gall bladder.
