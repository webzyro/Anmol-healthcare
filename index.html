Common Queries

Frequently Asked Questions by Patients

Get answers to the most common questions about our procedures, recovery, and clinic services.

Laparoscopic surgery, also known as minimally invasive surgery, involves making small incisions instead of one large one. A tiny camera (laparoscope) is inserted, allowing the surgeon to see and operate with high precision. This results in less pain, smaller scars, and a much faster recovery compared to traditional open surgery.

Most patients can return to light activities within 2-3 days after a laparoscopic stone procedure. Complete recovery typically takes about 1-2 weeks, depending on the complexity of the case. We provide detailed post-operative care instructions to ensure a smooth and speedy healing process.

Yes, we work with most major health insurance providers. Our administrative team will assist you with the pre-authorization process and paperwork to ensure a hassle-free experience. Please bring your insurance details during your initial consultation.

Laparoscopic surgery is generally very safe and carries lower risks of infection and bleeding compared to open surgery. However, as with any medical procedure, there are minor risks that our expert surgeons will discuss with you in detail during your consultation, taking into account your specific health condition.

For your first visit, please bring your medical history records, any previous scan reports (Ultrasound, CT, etc.), a list of current medications, and your ID proof. This helps our doctors provide the most accurate assessment and personalized treatment plan for you.
